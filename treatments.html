General Dentistry

General dentistry is a field that focuses on the prevention and treatment of tooth decay. The most common causes of tooth decay are primarily the consumption of a high-sugar diet, bacteria, and a lack of proper oral hygiene. The treatment of tooth decay involves the thorough removal of the "decayed" hard tissues of the tooth and the application of a filling that restores the lost part of the tooth. According to global standards, we use modern fillings such as porcelain, composite (micro and nanohybrid), and glass-ionomer from reputable companies. These materials have a wide range of colors and high mechanical strength, allowing us to faithfully and naturally reconstruct a damaged tooth. Thanks to the effective local anesthetics we use, these procedures are minimally discomforting for patients.

Dental Hygiene

Daily hygiene practices, adherence to recommendations, and regular visits to the hygiene office allow one to enjoy a beautiful, healthy smile throughout life. Regular check-up visits every 6 months enable the early detection and treatment of oral diseases, as well as the performance of professional hygiene procedures, including: Oral hygiene instructions, selection of brushing methods, and hygiene accessories. Professional teeth cleaning: Scaling: removal of mineralized dental plaque using ultrasonic devices. Sandblasting: removal of deposits and discolorations using a sandblaster. Polishing: smoothing the cleaned surfaces of the teeth. Fluoridation: the application of fluoride-containing preparations promotes enamel remineralization and strengthening, prevents tooth decay, and alleviates sensitivity.

Endodontics

Endodontics is a dental specialty focused on diagnosing and treating diseases of the dental pulp and tissues surrounding the tooth roots. Commonly known as root canal treatment, endodontic procedures involve removing infected or damaged pulp, cleaning and shaping the root canals, and sealing them to prevent further infection. This specialty aims to save natural teeth by addressing issues such as tooth decay, trauma, or infections that affect the innermost part of the tooth. Endodontists use advanced techniques, including microscopic and digital technologies, to perform precise and effective treatments. Successful endodontic care alleviates pain, preserves natural teeth, and contributes to overall oral health.

Prosthodontics

Prosthodontics deals with the reconstruction of lost tooth tissues using crowns, bridges, dentures, and implants. It also improves the aesthetic, cosmetic, and psychological impression of the patient. After the loss of natural teeth or extensive damage, prosthodontic treatment often becomes the last 'lifeline' for maintaining and extending a youthful appearance and radiant smile. With the experience and knowledge I possess, I can offer my patients various options for filling gaps or imperfections in their dentition. Together, we determine a detailed plan and the stages of treatment. We specialize in comprehensive prosthodontic treatment.

Dental Implants

Implantology is an ideal, nature-mimicking therapy that allows for the permanent restoration of missing teeth. It is possible to replace any lost tooth. Teeth lost due to accidents, periodontal disease, cavities, unsuccessful root canal treatment, or ineffective root apex resection can be replaced with an implant and a prosthetic crown. This ensures excellent aesthetic results and a natural appearance of the dentition, providing the patient with complete physical and psychological comfort, and avoiding issues associated with traditional prosthetic replacements, such as removable dentures or the need to grind healthy teeth.

Dental Surgery

Dental surgery is a specialised medical field focused on supporting various dental disciplines, such as endodontics (root canal treatment), orthodontics, periodontics (gum disease treatment), and prosthodontics (dental prosthetics). Ensuring the safety, health, and comfort of patients, our clinic utilises top-quality equipment and materials for all dental surgical procedures. This commitment extends to employing the latest technologies for optimal patient care. Common dental surgical interventions include extractions, implant placements, and other procedures aimed at maintaining or restoring oral health.
