Genral Dentistry

General dentistry is a field that focuses on the prevention and treatment of tooth decay. The most common causes of tooth decay are primarily the consumption of a high-sugar diet, bacteria, and a lack of proper oral hygiene. The treatment of tooth decay involves the thorough removal of the "decayed" hard tissues of the tooth and the application of a filling that restores the lost part of the tooth. According to global standards, we use modern fillings such as porcelain, composite (micro and nanohybrid), and glass-ionomer from reputable companies. These materials have a wide range of colors and high mechanical strength, allowing us to faithfully and naturally reconstruct a damaged tooth. Thanks to the effective local anesthetics we use, these procedures are minimally discomforting for patients.

Dental Hygiene

Dental hygiene is a field aimed at preventing oral diseases, especially tooth decay and periodontal diseases. When effectively conducted, it helps avoid prolonged and costly tooth treatments. Its primary task is to combat dental plaque, the main cause of oral issues. The guarantee of the effectiveness of preventive measures is the continuous collaboration between the patient and the dental office. Daily hygiene practices, adherence to recommendations, and regular visits to the hygiene office allow one to enjoy a beautiful, healthy smile throughout life. Regular check-up visits every 6 months enable the early detection and treatment of oral diseases, as well as the performance of professional hygiene procedures, including: Oral hygiene instructions, taking into account the patient's individual needs, selection of brushing methods, and hygiene accessories. Professional teeth cleaning: Scaling: removal of mineralized dental plaque using ultrasonic devices. Sandblasting: removal of deposits and discolorations using a sandblaster. Polishing: smoothing the cleaned surfaces of the teeth. Fluoridation: the application of fluoride-containing preparations promotes enamel remineralization and strengthening, prevents tooth decay, and alleviates sensitivity.

Endodontics

Endodontics deals with the diagnosis and treatment of pulp diseases in teeth and periapical tissues. Root canal treatment (endodontic treatment) is necessary when dead tissue or bacteria cause inflammation in the tooth chamber and root canals. The treating dentist thoroughly cleans and disinfects the tooth, and then fills the canals with an appropriate material. Remember! If pain or swelling occurs, it is crucial to react promptly

Prosthodontics

Prosthodontics deals with the reconstruction of lost tooth tissues using crowns, bridges, dentures, and implants. It also improves the aesthetic, cosmetic, and psychological impression of the patient. After the loss of natural teeth or extensive damage, prosthodontic treatment often becomes the last 'lifeline' for maintaining and extending a youthful appearance and radiant smile. With the experience and knowledge I possess, I can offer my patients various options for filling gaps or imperfections in their dentition. Together, we determine a detailed plan and the stages of treatment. We specialize in comprehensive prosthodontic treatment.

Dental Implants

Implantology is an ideal, nature-mimicking therapy that allows for the permanent restoration of missing teeth. It is possible to replace any lost tooth. Teeth lost due to accidents, periodontal disease, cavities, unsuccessful root canal treatment, or ineffective root apex resection can be replaced with an implant and a prosthetic crown. This ensures excellent aesthetic results and a natural appearance of the dentition, providing the patient with complete physical and psychological comfort, and avoiding issues associated with traditional prosthetic replacements, such as removable dentures or the need to grind healthy teeth.

Dental Surgery

Dental surgery is a medical specialty aimed at supporting other fields of dentistry, such as endodontics (root canal treatment), orthodontics, periodontics (treatment of gum diseases), and prosthodontics. Procedures within its scope require a high level of precision. They are performed under local anesthesia, and if necessary, under general anesthesia. With a focus on the safety, health, and comfort of our patients, all oral surgery procedures are conducted using top-quality equipment and materials in the comfortable conditions of our clinic.
